Our History

Steinway & Sons, also known as Steinway, is an American piano company founded in 1853 in Manhattan by German piano builder Heinrich Engelhard Steinweg (later known as Henry E. Steinway). The company's growth led to the opening of a factory in New York City, United States, and later, a factory in Hamburg, Germany. Steinway pianos have set an uncompromising standard for sound, touch, beauty, and investment value.

Piano Models

Model D Concert Grand

The Model D is the pinnacle of concert grands and is widely regarded as the finest concert piano in the world. It's the choice of the majority of concert artists.

Model B Music Room Grand

Often referred to as the "perfect piano," it's a wonderfully balanced and versatile instrument that does extremely well in intimate settings, teaching studios, and mid-sizedvenues.

Model S Baby Grand

The Model S is the smallest of Steinway's grand pianos. It's known for its charming quality and rich tone despite its more compact size. A perfect addition to any room.

Craftsmanship

Every Steinway & Sons piano is a testament to our commitment to craftsmanship. Over 12,000 individual parts are meticulously crafted to produce instruments known for their rich, resonant tone and responsive touch. Our pianos continue to be the overwhelming choice of artists, institutions, and piano enthusiasts around the world.
